CS6457 – Video Game Design

This course challenged us to apply principles of game mechanics, interactivity, and user engagement to build a full-featured digital game from scratch.

Overview

As part of the final project, my team and I designed and built an original 3D RPG game using Unity. The game world featured handcrafted environments, unique characters, and a narrative that gave players meaningful decisions throughout their progression. We handled everything from art and sound integration to implementing gameplay logic and balancing combat systems.

Design & Development

I was primarily responsible for implementing player and enemy mechanics. This included character movement, jumping, health systems, and enemy AI. The enemy AI was designed to use behavior trees and finite-state logic to switch between states like patrolling, chasing, or attacking the player based on proximity and game context.

I also created UI elements like health bars, menus, and feedback systems. These helped tie the experience together and make the game feel more polished and intuitive.

Testing & Feedback

We regularly tested the game to ensure playability and fun. Playtesters gave us insight into pacing, challenge level, and confusion points. Based on this, we rebalanced certain enemies, tweaked collision hitboxes, and improved tutorials and in-game hints.

What I Learned

Game design isn’t just about ideas—it’s about communication, iteration, and attention to how players experience your work. This project taught me how to bring a creative vision to life through technical implementation, and how to receive and incorporate feedback constructively. It also made me appreciate just how much effort goes into tuning the little details that make games feel right.
